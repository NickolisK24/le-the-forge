# v4.5B.6 Coverage and Confidence Systems

## Architectural Purpose

v4.5B.6 establishes deterministic governance-safe public coverage and
confidence visibility systems on top of the v4.5A governance-safe drift
intelligence chain, v4.5B.1 trust visibility foundations, v4.5B.2 support
status visibility, v4.5B.3 explainability surfaces, v4.5B.4 provenance and
lineage visibility, and v4.5B.5 deterministic evidence panels.

The phase makes coverage and confidence visibility public for support coverage,
evidence coverage, explainability coverage, provenance coverage, lineage
coverage, continuity coverage, diagnostics coverage, incomplete coverage,
unknown coverage, and fail-visible coverage diagnostics.

## Deterministic Coverage Philosophy

Coverage visibility describes the presence, partial presence, unsupported
state, incomplete state, blocked state, stale state, or unknown state of public
trust visibility surfaces.

Coverage visibility is descriptive-only. It does not approve, authorize,
recommend, rank, score, execute, enable production use, or imply operational
readiness.

## Deterministic Confidence Philosophy

Confidence visibility describes whether confidence is evidence-supported,
partially supported, continuity-supported, provenance-supported,
lineage-supported, unsupported, incomplete, or unknown.

Confidence visibility does not create trust scoring, evidence scoring, ranking,
recommendation, authorization, approval, execution enablement, operational
readiness, execution safety, or production enablement.

## Support Coverage Philosophy

Support coverage visibility describes fully covered support states, partially
covered support states, unsupported coverage states, incomplete coverage states,
blocked coverage states, and unknown coverage states.

Support coverage remains visibility only and does not create recommendations or
approval semantics.

## Evidence Coverage Philosophy

Evidence coverage visibility describes complete evidence coverage, partial
evidence coverage, unsupported evidence coverage, stale evidence coverage,
unknown evidence coverage, and incomplete evidence coverage.

Evidence coverage remains descriptive-only. It does not create trust scoring,
evidence ranking, evidence scoring, authorization, approval, or operational
behavior.

## Explainability Coverage Philosophy

Explainability coverage visibility describes complete explainability coverage,
partial explainability coverage, unsupported explainability coverage,
incomplete explainability coverage, continuity explainability coverage, and
diagnostics explainability coverage.

Explainability coverage does not create operational semantics or
recommendation behavior.

## Provenance and Lineage Coverage Philosophy

Provenance and lineage coverage visibility describes complete provenance
coverage, partial provenance coverage, stale provenance coverage, unknown
provenance coverage, complete lineage coverage, partial lineage coverage, and
unsupported lineage coverage.

Visibility does not grant source authority, ranking behavior, recommendation
behavior, scoring semantics, authorization, or approval.

## Incomplete and Unknown Coverage Philosophy

Incomplete support coverage, incomplete evidence coverage, incomplete
explainability coverage, incomplete provenance coverage, incomplete lineage
coverage, unknown confidence visibility, and unknown coverage visibility remain
explicit and fail-visible.

Incomplete and unknown states are not suppressed, silently replaced, converted
to trust scores, or treated as operational fallback behavior.

## Fail-Visible Coverage Diagnostics Philosophy

Fail-visible coverage diagnostics expose missing coverage references,
incomplete support coverage, incomplete evidence coverage, incomplete
explainability coverage, incomplete provenance coverage, incomplete lineage
coverage, unsupported confidence states, unknown coverage states, inherited
coverage limitation gaps, and inherited coverage prohibition gaps.

Diagnostics remain descriptive-only. They do not remediate, repair, mitigate,
correct, approve, authorize, rank, recommend, score, execute, route, traverse,
dispatch, schedule, sequence, integrate planners, consume production paths, or
mutate runtime state.

confidence visibility records, support coverage records, evidence coverage

Hashes are stable, deterministic, replay-safe, provenance-safe, lineage-safe,
and integrity-safe.
