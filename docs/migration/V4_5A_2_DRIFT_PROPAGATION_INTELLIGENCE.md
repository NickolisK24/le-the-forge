# v4.5A.2 Drift Propagation Intelligence

## Architectural Purpose

v4.5A.2 establishes deterministic governance-safe drift propagation intelligence
on top of the v4.5A.1 drift foundation infrastructure. The phase models
propagation chains, inherited propagation, refinement propagation,
cross-boundary propagation, continuity propagation, severity accumulation,
evidence chains, explainability visibility, and fail-visible propagation
diagnostics.

This phase is descriptive-only governance intelligence. It does not introduce
runtime orchestration, planner execution, remediation, repair, mitigation,
propagation correction, propagation suppression, production consumption, or
runtime mutation.

## Deterministic Propagation Intelligence Foundations

The propagation layer represents:

- deterministic propagation identity records
- deterministic direct, inherited, refinement, cross-boundary, segmentation,
  compatibility, continuity, and explainability propagation visibility
- deterministic severity accumulation visibility for isolated, localized,
  distributed, cross-boundary, continuity-impacting, and integrity-impacting
  propagation
- replay-safe and provenance-safe propagation evidence chains
- deterministic continuity and lineage references
- deterministic cross-boundary visibility across governance scopes,
  inheritance chains, refinement chains, segmentation boundaries, and
  explainability domains

All records are immutable and are exported through canonical deterministic JSON
serialization before hashing.

## Propagation Explainability Philosophy

Propagation explainability remains explainability-only. The implementation
surfaces propagation origin, chain, inherited propagation, refinement
propagation, continuity impact, unsupported propagation, and fail-visible
diagnostic visibility without activating operational response.

No explainability record authorizes recommendations, decisions, routing,
dispatch, traversal, scheduling, sequencing, remediation, mitigation, repair, or
propagation suppression.

## Fail-Visible Propagation Diagnostics

The diagnostic layer explicitly models:

- unresolved propagation chains
- incomplete propagation lineage
- continuity gaps
- unsupported propagation states
- missing propagation evidence
- incompatible propagation inheritance
- explainability discontinuities
- propagation integrity gaps

Diagnostics are fail-visible and descriptive-only. They do not suppress
propagation, repair lineage, correct evidence, mitigate drift, or trigger
orchestration response.

## Continuity Guarantees

v4.5A.2 preserves deterministic visibility for propagation chain continuity,
lineage continuity, provenance continuity, evidence continuity, and integrity
continuity. Continuity records remain replay-safe, rollback-safe, lineage-safe,
provenance-safe, and integrity-safe.

Continuity visibility does not imply runtime traversal, orchestration
semantics, planner authority, or operational behavior.

## Hashing Guarantees

The hashing layer provides stable SHA-256 hashes for:

- propagation identity
- propagation chains
- propagation classifications
- evidence chains
- continuity records
- severity accumulation records
- explainability visibility
- cross-boundary visibility
- propagation diagnostics
- unsupported propagation visibility
- the full propagation intelligence payload

Hashes are stable, deterministic, replay-safe, and integrity-safe.

## Serialization Guarantees

- deterministic ordering

Collection ordering is normalized through deterministic ordering keys before
serialization.
