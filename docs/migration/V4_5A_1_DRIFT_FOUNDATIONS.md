# v4.5A.1 Drift Foundations

## Architectural Purpose

v4.5A.1 establishes deterministic governance-safe drift foundation
infrastructure for EpochForge Track A. The phase models boundary drift identity,
classification, lineage, evidence references, severity visibility, continuity
visibility, explainability foundations, and fail-visible diagnostics.

This phase is descriptive-only intelligence infrastructure. It does not change
planner behavior, production consumption, runtime orchestration, remediation,
repair, correction, authorization, routing, dispatch, scheduling, sequencing, or
runtime state.

## Governance-Safe Drift Foundations

The drift foundation layer represents:

- deterministic drift identity records for source, inherited, refinement,
  governance scope, and continuity chain identifiers
- deterministic drift classifications for inheritance, refinement,
  segmentation, scope, compatibility, continuity, explainability, and integrity
  visibility drift
- replay-safe and provenance-safe drift evidence references
- deterministic continuity visibility for lineage, provenance, inheritance,
  refinement, governance, and integrity continuity
- deterministic severity visibility for informational, low, moderate, high,
  and critical visibility levels
- explicit unsupported operational state visibility

All records are immutable, serialized through canonical JSON ordering, and
hashed with stable SHA-256 evidence.

## Descriptive-Only Guarantees

v4.5A.1 remains:

- NON-operational
- NON-authorizing
- NON-executing
- NON-remediating
- NON-runtime-mutating

Severity, classification, diagnostics, and unsupported-state records are
visibility signals only. They do not authorize remediation, repair, automated
correction, orchestration response, planner execution, or operational behavior.

## Fail-Visible Philosophy

Unsupported and incomplete states are preserved explicitly. The implementation
models unresolved drift, incomplete lineage, missing evidence, incompatible
refinement, continuity gaps, explainability gaps, and unsupported drift states
as fail-visible diagnostics.

No fallback inference is used. No unsupported state is silently normalized into
a supported state.

## Continuity Guarantees

The foundation layer preserves deterministic visibility for:

- lineage continuity
- provenance continuity
- inheritance continuity
- refinement continuity
- governance continuity
- integrity continuity

Continuity visibility does not repair continuity gaps, remediate drift, or
mutate runtime or operational state.

## Hashing Guarantees

The hashing layer provides stable SHA-256 hashes for:

- drift foundation identity
- drift identity records
- drift classification records
- evidence references
- continuity visibility
- diagnostics
- severity visibility
- unsupported-state visibility
- the full drift foundation payload

Hashes are deterministic, replay-safe, rollback-safe, and integrity-safe.

## Serialization Guarantees

The serialization layer preserves:

- ordering stability
- replayability
- provenance continuity
- explicit unsupported-state visibility
- deterministic canonical JSON output

Collection ordering is normalized through deterministic ordering keys before
serialization.

v4.5A.1 preserves the inherited governance prohibitions:

- no orchestration authorization

- no orchestration routing
- no orchestration traversal
- no orchestration scheduling
- no orchestration sequencing
- no orchestration recommendations
- no orchestration decisions
- no remediation systems
- no repair systems
- no automated corrections

- no operational mutation

- no production consumption
- no runtime orchestration semantics

## Unsupported Operational States

Unsupported operational states remain explicit report evidence:

- orchestration execution
- orchestration activation
- orchestration authorization
- orchestration routing
- orchestration scheduling
- remediation
- repair
- automated correction
- runtime mutation
- planner execution
- operational behavior

These are unsupported states, not activation targets.

## Limitations Intentionally Preserved

v4.5A.1 intentionally does not:

- remediate, repair, resolve, or correct drift
- execute or activate orchestration responses
- authorize or approve orchestration
- route, dispatch, traverse, schedule, or sequence orchestration
- recommend, rank, score, select, optimize, or decide orchestration
- integrate planners or execute planner behavior
- consume production bundles
- mutate runtime or operational state

This phase only establishes deterministic governance-safe drift foundations for
later descriptive intelligence expansion.
