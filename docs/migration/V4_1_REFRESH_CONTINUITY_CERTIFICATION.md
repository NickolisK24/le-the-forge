# v4.1 Refresh Continuity Certification

Phase 9 adds deterministic refresh continuity certification governance for the
entire v4.1 refresh stack. The certification layer models continuity evidence
for manifests, dependencies, lineage, schema evolution, sequencing, drift,
replay, rollback, diagnostics, and explainability.

This phase is descriptive-only certification infrastructure. It does not repair
continuity gaps and it does not authorize operational behavior.

## Scope

- Deterministic continuity identity.
- Manifest continuity certification.
- Dependency continuity certification.
- Lineage continuity certification.
- Schema continuity certification.
- Sequencing continuity certification.
- Drift continuity certification.
- Replay continuity certification.
- Rollback continuity certification.
- Diagnostics continuity certification.
- Explainability continuity certification.
- Cross-layer continuity aggregation.
- Cross-layer continuity diagnostics.
- Cross-layer continuity failure visibility.
- Unsupported, blocked, prohibited, and stale continuity-state visibility.
- Deterministic serialization, hashing, equality, and integrity auditing.

## Explicit Limitations

- No remediation exists.
- No automatic correction exists.
- No recommendation ranking scoring or selection exists.
- No approval or authorization exists.
- No orchestration execution exists.
- No automatic sequencing exists.
- No refresh execution exists.
- No migration execution exists.
- No recovery execution exists.
- No rollback execution exists.
- No planner integration exists.
- No production consumption exists.
- No mutation behavior exists.

## Explicit Prohibitions

- No continuity certification becomes operational authorization.
- No continuity certification implies approval capability.
- No automatic continuity correction exists.
- No remediation, correction, repair, recommendation, ranking, scoring,
  selection, approval, authorization, orchestration, or execution pathway is
  introduced.
- No hidden operational assumptions are introduced.

## Fail-Visible Warnings

The default Phase 9 evidence intentionally includes fail-visible examples for
each continuity layer, plus unsupported provider, blocked state, prohibited
domain, stale evidence, and cross-layer conflict visibility. These are
diagnostic evidence states only. They do not trigger repair, recovery, approval,
or execution behavior.
