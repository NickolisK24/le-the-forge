# v4.3 Closeout And v4.4 Readiness

## Architectural Purpose

Phase 10 closes out v4.3 by certifying that the complete orchestration governance modeling chain from Phases 1-9 is deterministic, replay-safe, rollback-safe, lineage-safe, provenance-safe, integrity-safe, and ready for v4.4 planning.

This is descriptive-only orchestration governance closeout certification. It is not operational orchestration authorization.

## What v4.3 Accomplished

v4.3 established deterministic governance-safe orchestration modeling across:

- manifest foundations
- topology visibility
- boundary and capability visibility
- policy visibility
- transition visibility
- coordination visibility
- diagnostics and explainability aggregation
- continuity and integrity certification
- readiness certification

Together these phases make orchestration governance evidence visible, auditable, serializable, hashable, explainable, replay-safe, rollback-safe, and fail-visible without creating an orchestration engine.

## How The Phases Connect

Phase 1 created deterministic manifest identity and governance boundaries.

Phase 2 modeled structural topology relationships without traversal.

Phase 3 modeled capabilities and governance boundaries without activation.

Phase 4 modeled policy constraints without enforcement or authorization.

Phase 5 modeled theoretical transitions without transition execution or state progression.

Phase 6 modeled coordination relationships without dispatch or runtime coordination.

Phase 7 aggregated diagnostics and explainability across all prior visibility layers without recommendations or decisions.

Phase 8 certified continuity, integrity, lineage, provenance, replay safety, and rollback safety without authorization.

Phase 9 certified architectural closeout planning readiness without operational readiness approval.

Phase 10 certifies the whole v4.3 chain for closeout and v4.4 planning readiness.

## What Remains Prohibited

v4.3 intentionally prohibits:

- orchestration execution
- orchestration activation
- orchestration authorization
- orchestration approval
- orchestration runtime behavior
- orchestration dispatch
- orchestration routing
- orchestration traversal
- orchestration scheduling
- orchestration sequencing
- orchestration decisions
- orchestration recommendations
- ranking, scoring, selection, and optimization
- orchestration planning engines
- orchestration decision engines
- planner integration
- production consumption
- remediation
- repair
- inference
- runtime mutation
- operational mutation
- hidden orchestration pathways
- implicit operational authorization

No orchestration engine, orchestration runtime, or authorization pathway exists after v4.3 closeout.
