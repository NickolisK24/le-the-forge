# v3.9 Transition Scenario Intelligence

## What Phase 6 Adds

v3.9 Phase 6 models hypothetical transition scenarios from deterministic transition session evidence.

It records scenario variants, descriptive risks, non-selective comparisons, findings, evidence, provenance, continuity, and visibility without enabling execution behavior.

This phase does NOT enable orchestration execution, traversal, routing, scheduling, dispatch, optimization, recommendation, ranking, scoring, selection, authorization, approval, or runtime mutation.

## Scenarios Are Not Execution

A transition scenario is a deterministic evidence model. It does not execute a transition, authorize a transition, traverse a graph, choose a route, schedule dispatch, approve behavior, mutate runtime state, or produce a production pathway.

## Hypothetical Variants

Scenario variants are immutable evidence references derived from session evidence. They preserve deterministic ordering, provenance references, continuity references, and explainability references.

## Non-Selective Comparisons

Scenario comparisons can describe matching evidence, differing evidence, missing evidence, continuity differences, provenance differences, explainability differences, risk differences, and classification differences.

Comparisons do not choose a best scenario, rank scenarios, score scenarios, recommend scenarios, optimize selection, or imply approval.

## Descriptive Risks

Scenario risks are descriptive evidence records only. They do not contain numeric rankings, priority scores, recommendation outputs, or selection outcomes.

## Fail-Visible Findings

Every scenario finding includes a reason, evidence reference, provenance reference, continuity reference, and explainability message.

No scenario state, risk, or comparison is hidden, silently corrected, or promoted from partial or non-modeled to modeled.

## Governance-Safe Scenario Tracking

Governance and integrity findings can block a scenario while preserving deterministic evidence, provenance, replay, rollback, and explainability continuity.

## Builds On Session Intelligence

Phase 6 consumes Phase 5 transition session outputs as scenario evidence references and packages hypothetical variants without changing session reasoning.
